# HatchKit.ai Marketing Website PRD

## Current Direction

HatchKit.ai is the public sales site for HatchKit, software that handles breeder-business busywork.
The site should convert qualified breeder operators into demo requests by making the
business value clear: capture leads, organize buyers, review shipping safety, plan
content, and create repeat-customer workflows.

## Positioning

HatchKit is business software for breeders. It is not a pet store brand, not a cute
animal site, and not a generic CRM with breeder language pasted on top.

Primary brand feel:

- Smart
- Clean
- Organized
- Operational
- Safe movement and shipping
- Social media simplicity

## Target Visitors

Primary visitors are breeders and breeder-business operators who sell through a mix of
events, websites, social media, marketplaces, local pickup, or shipping. The site should
work for reptile breeders as an early audience without visually or verbally becoming
reptile-only.

## Conversion Goals

1. Get the visitor to book a demo or talk to Brianna/the HatchKit team.
2. Explain HatchKit in plain breeder language: "we handle the business busywork."
3. Build trust through Brianna/YetiGex as proof that the system came from a real breeder business.
4. Make shipping safety, follow-up, website, email, social media, CRM, and sales tracking feel like practical help, not abstract software.
5. Capture qualified leads from visitors who are not ready to book immediately.

## Copy Strategy

The core buyer is often a self-employed breeder or animal seller, not a technical SaaS buyer.
Use plain language:

- "Stop losing show leads."
- "Know who wanted what."
- "No more guessing on shipping day."
- "Stop staring at a blank caption box."
- "We set it up with you."
- "You do not need to become a tech person."

Avoid sounding like a corporate software brochure. Terms like CRM, automation, pipeline,
and AI are useful only when paired with a plain-English explanation.

## Required Pages

- `/`: Core landing page with plain-language busywork hero, video, workflow modules, pricing, FAQ, and final CTA.
- `/features`: Full feature detail for lead capture, buyer inbox, sales pipeline, shipping safety, content, media, animal profiles, follow-up, website/funnels, and reporting.
- `/pricing`: Plan cards, comparison table, pricing FAQ, and demo CTA.
- `/about`: HatchKit story, principles, audience fit, and trust positioning.
- `/demo`: Demo request form and call expectations.
- `/show`: Event/show lead capture funnel.
- `/privacy` and `/terms`: HatchKit legal pages.

## Content Standards

- Say HatchKit, not old product names.
- Avoid old green/orange visual language.
- Avoid cute-animal or pet-store framing.
- Keep CTAs direct: demo, features, pricing.
- Make pages useful to a buyer deciding whether to request a call.
