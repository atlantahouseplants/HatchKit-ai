# HatchKit.ai — Marketing Website PRD

## Product Requirements Document
**Version 1.0 | March 2026**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the requirements for the HatchKit.ai public marketing website. This is the primary sales tool for the HatchKit business — it needs to take a reptile breeder who has never heard of HatchKit from "what is this?" to "I want a demo" in under 3 minutes.

The website will be built as a static site (Next.js or plain HTML/CSS/JS) deployed to Vercel at hatchkit.ai.

---

## 2. Target Audience

The primary visitor is a mid-tier reptile breeder ($20K–$150K/year revenue) who:
- Attends reptile expos and sells through MorphMarket + social media
- Has no CRM, no marketing automation, no systematic follow-up
- Is NOT technical — they can use Instagram but couldn't set up a Mailchimp account
- Was either referred by another breeder or met our sales partner at a show
- Is skeptical of "tech solutions" because they've been burned by tools that don't understand their niche

Secondary visitors include feeder insect sellers, snake breeders, and other exotic animal vendors.

---

## 3. Site Goals (Ranked)

1. **Book a demo call** — this is the #1 conversion goal. Every page should have a clear path to booking.
2. **Build credibility** — prove that HatchKit was built by people who understand the reptile breeding business, not generic SaaS people.
3. **Explain the product** — clearly communicate what HatchKit does across its 6 modules.
4. **Differentiate from competitors** — make it obvious that this is NOT another husbandry tracker. This is a growth engine.
5. **Capture leads** — for visitors not ready to book a demo, collect email/phone for nurture.

## 5. Design Requirements

### Brand Identity
- **Primary color:** Deep forest green (#1B5E20) — trust, nature, reptile connection
- **Secondary color:** Warm amber/orange (#E65100) — energy, growth, warmth, egg yolk
- **Accent:** Bright amber (#FF6F00) — CTAs, highlights
- **Neutrals:** Near-black (#1A1A1A) for text, warm gray (#F5F5F0) for backgrounds
- **Dark sections:** Use deep green or near-black for contrast sections

### Typography
- **Headlines:** A bold, distinctive display font — not generic (consider: Clash Display, Cabinet Grotesk, Satoshi, or General Sans)
- **Body:** A clean, highly readable sans-serif (consider: DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, or Outfit)
- **Avoid:** Inter, Arial, Roboto, system fonts — these scream "generic SaaS template"

### Visual Style
- Modern and professional but warm — not cold corporate SaaS
- Should feel like it belongs in the reptile community, not Silicon Valley
- Use real reptile imagery where possible (stock photos of geckos, snakes, show floors)
- Custom illustrations or icons for the 6 modules preferred over generic icon libraries
- Subtle textures or patterns that evoke scales, eggs, or natural elements
- No generic gradient blobs or abstract shapes that scream "AI startup template"

### Motion & Interaction
- Smooth scroll animations for sections appearing on scroll
- Subtle hover effects on cards and buttons
- The AI shipping agent section could have a mini animated flow showing the decision process
- Keep it fast — no heavy animations that slow page load
- Mobile-first responsive design — many breeders will visit on their phone from a show

### Photography Direction
If using images:
- Real breeder workspaces (rack systems, incubators, shipping setups)
- Healthy, beautiful reptiles in good lighting
- Show floor environments
- Hands holding geckos/snakes (shows the human element)
- NEVER: generic stock photos of people pointing at screens, handshakes, or meaningless abstract imagery

## 6. Technical Requirements

### Stack
- **Framework:** Next.js (static export) or plain HTML/Tailwind — keep it simple and fast
- **Hosting:** Vercel at hatchkit.ai
- **Analytics:** Plausible or simple Google Analytics
- **Forms:** Either embedded GHL form (for lead capture that goes directly into your CRM) or a simple form that posts to a webhook
- **Calendar:** Embedded Calendly or GHL calendar widget on /demo page
- **Performance:** Target 95+ Lighthouse score — fast load times matter for mobile visitors at shows with spotty wifi

### SEO Basics
- Proper meta titles and descriptions on every page
- Open Graph tags for social sharing
- Semantic HTML (proper heading hierarchy)
- Alt text on all images
- Sitemap.xml
- Target keywords: "reptile breeder CRM," "reptile shipping software," "breeder marketing automation," "reptile business tools"

### Mobile Requirements
- Fully responsive, mobile-first
- Touch-friendly buttons and navigation
- Fast load on 4G connections
- Sticky CTA button on mobile (always visible "Book Demo" button)
